CHECKLIST

Building a home can be one the biggest undertakings of a lifetime, and although a basic checklist and general list of questions won’t cover every aspect, it will help you to weigh the decision and set your plans in motion

1) Affordability

When choosing the house plans and lot, the reality of what you can afford obviously takes precedence. But you shouldn’t stretch your budget thin. In construction, there almost certainly will be unforeseen expenses, such as land grading or groundwater issues that can hinder the home’s completion schedule and cost.

2) Location

Before settling on a parcel of land, consider things like where the school or city bus stops and the distance to schools, stores and to your place of employment

3) House Plans

When selecting a home by looking at plans on a computer screen or in a book or magazine, you won’t get an optimal idea of size and layout. Envision the potential home by comparing its size and layout to your current home’s size and layout. Ask yourself if the home has enough or too many windows on the sunny side, if the kitchen is large enough and flows nicely, if the home offers plenty of storage, closets and a usable attic; some things are changeable -- at a cost. 

4) The Contractor

The right contractor can be one of your biggest allies. Before hiring anyone,ask him to direct you to samples of his work and to explain his warranty. Ask how much money he expects up front, which may be a modest down payment but should be never more than half; your bank may only release the funds in partial increments, so ask your lender about payment arrangements. Obtain a copy of the contract.

5) Visit frequently

Visit the construction site regularly to make sure that the correct materials, colors and fixtures are being used. You’ll sleep better throughout the building process when you’re armed with as much knowledge about it as possible.
